## Details

### Wing Jig

Inspired by a fellow builder, I decided to make a jig to carry the wing flat to make the mounting easier later.

#### Preparing

The materials:

- 2x4x8ft wood from Home Depot
- Deck screws
- 3m woolen mat from Amazon
- 4x Trailer Jack with Wheel from Amazon

The idea is: the wing should lay flat on the jig, and the 4 legs are all height adjustable to I can wiggle the height when aligning the wing to the fuselage.

The total length of the jig is roughly 8 foot. One side (root) is wider than the other side (mid span of the wing) considering the taper of the wing. Also, the wider side is lower than the the other due to the dihedral wing design.

I stetched the design and calculated the height difference between the low side and the high side based on the presumed 8ft length and dihedral degrees from the POH. Gemini told me it's supposed to be 210mm difference.

### Bullets

In order to help punch the AN7 bolts through the wing spar, I made 2 bullets from sacrificia AN7 bolts. A bullet is essentially a tapered AN7 bolt. It's grinded thinner than a real AN bolt and therefore easier to insert into the bolt location. I can use it to temporarily align the bolt holes, install the bolts, then just punch it out.

The process is fairly straightforward - it's just a lot of grinding on my bench grinder. There is not much precision, just turn the bolt around and grind as long as the bolt is still cool enough for my hands to hold it.
