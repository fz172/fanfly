### Left Wing Mounting

Today is the big day. I got help from a bunch of hangar neighbors and my EAA chapter friends to mount the left wing.

The process is about the same as the right wing, but instead of 3 people around the wing I got about 10 people this time. And it really helped! Last time when we did the right wing it took us about 4 hours to get 5 bolts in, and this time it took us 4 maybe 5 to get all the bolts in.

In the morning we pushed the airplane out and got the left wing to sit on the cradle. Then we moved the airplane back in and started to align the wing with main spar.

As I moved through each bolt, I put the next one into dry ice to cool it down. So that's about 5 minutes of dry ice time on each bolt.

![](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AP1GczNPtXOS-MpyIBcq9uUg4OKbpgSXCO0fUrRJM3KeNUd_z9ijUzc5sRY5oIBFXrHgfCKw9oCeBDW3JCEDtOMhCIjp-n59-vBME2r6e1Tk7df818XXC82k4ypsmWURXkBqez0KuGxxPhhUDNiJkYQqvi-3VQ=w1764-h2352-s-no-gm?authuser=0)

We started the work around 10, and by 11:45am we got 7 out of the 8 bolts in although they are not fully through. I got them through far enough that the threads are not stuck in main spar so each bolt had 1-2 millimeter more to pull.

![](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AP1GczPbe-xghxsb2j7R_q5ZJd4xfbFZfEC5c8BKrVYJL2HHx_uPokbT-hrapaczdiR5JxqtkNRyg2qukS2GfUG9y6lK66j7NZzT8SyyxZUK7NjaV_l9MXwQJhzgZQPYzVLPoAu_pl_LSrI2oxh49eRdZ6Sb8A=w3136-h2352-s-no-gm?authuser=0)

At this point my helpers' jobs are done. I worked alone to drive the last bolt in. This one is at the lower left corner, close to the skin and very difficult to get the tools in for hammering or rivet gun tapping. But eventually I got enough force to drive in in about half way, then did the rest with rivet gun once the space was big enough.

Then I used a regular AN7 nut to pull every bolt through fully, and removed the nut.

Lastly, I put in the washer and the locknut and tightened them enough to feel the friction increase. I did not get ot torque them down yet. I will do it next.

### Right Aileron Mounting

I did not plan to do the right aileron, but I got a group of young visitors to tha hangar to help. They are 10-12 year old kids looking for aviation field experience. Edwin (fellow EAA chapter member), their advisor, told me they are experienced enough to work on some tasks. So I decided to let them help mounting the right aileron.

The mounting process is relatively easy. I already have the bolts, nuts, washers prepared for the kids. They needed to align the aileron's 2 hinges with the aileron mounting bearing on the wing, then insert the bolt, washer and lock it down with a locknut. I did not request them to torque the nut - wanted to do it myself once I inspect their work.

It took the kids a while to orient the parts to the right direction, and get the washers in place. But in the end they did a great job installing the aileron!
