## TLDR

- Installed camlocs
- Started to fit inspection doors

## Details

### Camlocs

Tools:

- Snap ring tool
- Camloc pliers
- Countersink bit and 3.2mm countersink rivets.

I match drilled the camloc holes on both the upper and lower cowling, and installed the camlocs.

To make sure the precision is high, I fully installed camloc one at a time before drilling the next hole. So this process took quite a while.

![](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/AP1GczNVqsZKh7pTl99izm5h7wtOofghguzZTHtBaji1yU51Ug3JNBJ-eqb1THH2D-2Yaygm_Wl72izOBBfAiWALENXqMKDQf73h0mY10MHEHI9dLSGDOk0Bm7eU5YNZ3yYkfe7xPBdtRXj1EVbQ7b8062Q2jQ=w1709-h1282-s-no-gm?authuser=0)

Unfortunately, once I installed everything the bottom spacing between the cowling and the propeller hub became 4.8mm, which was less than the minimum 5mm.

I have to sand off the tip of the cowling a little. In order to preserve the strength, I will add some fiberglass+epoxy mix at the back.

### Inspection Doors

After working on the whole cowling, the inspection door did not seem very difficult to do.

I placed the door on top of the cowling for a test fit first. There were some obvious places to be trimmed, which I marked out with a Sharpie.
